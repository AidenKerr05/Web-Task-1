Eglinton Castle

Shown on the right here is the ruins of Eglinton Castle.
Englinton Castle is actually technically in a town called Kilwinning, but it is very close to Irvine.
The castle was built in the 18th century and was the home of the Montgomerie family.
The castle was destroyed by fire in 1920 and has been a ruin ever since.
The castle is now a public park and is a popular place for walks and picnics.
Robert Burns, the famous Scottish poet, was a friend of the Montgomerie family and he wrote a poem about the castle.

Irvine Beach

Shown on the left here is Irvine Beach.
Irvine Beach is a long sandy beach that is popular with locals and tourists.
The beach is a great place for a walk and is also popular with surfers.
The beach is also home to Irvine Beach Park, which is a popular place for families.
The beach has a famous dragon stone which can be located on the top of a hill at the beach.
